C# Output

 WriteLine()
 - The WriteLine() is called a method.
 - To output values or print text in C#, you can use the WriteLine() method:
    Console.WriteLine("Hello World");
 - WriteLine() will add a new line for each method.

 Write()
 - Write() method does not insert a new line at the end of the output:
    Console.Write("Hello World! ");
    Console.Write("I will print on the same line.");
 
 - The data that's printed is sent in between the opening and closing parenthesis
   as an input parameter.
 - 
 - The parentheses () are known as the method invocation operator.
- The Console part is called a class. 
- Classes "own" methods. Methods live inside of a class.
